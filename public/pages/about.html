ABOUT

I am a person who is interested in all aspects of information technology. I believe that the greatest things ever will be the product of the efforts of people in this field. This is a very special time in human history and I am thrilled to be a part of it.

I studied Economics at Georgetown University and I continue to be interested in that field. I felt, however, that I could bring more value to the world by making software and making things more efficient generally. I enjoy making software that, pardon the cliche, makes a difference.

I recently finished a web development bootcamp, App Academy, in San Francisco. I blogged about it here. This was one of the hardest things I have done, and I definitely learned a lot. My knowledge prior was learned both on-the-job and by myself. App Academy has brought clarity and skill to areas where I previously felt less skilled.

Before completing App Academy, I worked at MTAG Services, first as an Analyst in their Acquisitions Department, and then as a (junior) Software Developer in their Information Technology Department. MTAG is a financial servicer and I hope to one day return to the financial services field in some capacity. Their business is very complex and niche, and in my time there I came to mostly understand this complexity. I would not necessarily be daunted to see a large codebase or org chart or process diagram again. Please see my CV (pdf) for more information on my background.
